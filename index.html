Statement
Timothy, Lake Brienz, CH

The people in these places carry more than most of us ask them to: the labor behind informal economies, herding routes reshaped by retreating glaciers, fishing and tourism livelihoods tied to oceans that don't behave the way they used to. My approach is simple, even if I'm still learning to do it well: a camera, real time spent in a place, and the same careful visual language applied everywhere, so no location is dressed up or down to seem more important than another. I name people where I can, with consent, and I'm working toward letting their own words sit in this space alongside mine, instead of standing in for them.

Knowledge is most powerful when it illuminates human stories: the kind data alone can't carry. That's what this practice is trying to do: make the realities of climate-impacted communities visible, so their experience can inform something beyond a photograph.
